Mudanjiang Overview

Geographic Location

Mudanjiang is located in southeastern Heilongjiang Province, with geographic coordinates between 128°02′–131°18′E and 43°24′–45°59′N. It borders Russia's Far East to the east, Yanbian Korean Autonomous Prefecture of Jilin Province to the south, Harbin to the west, and Jiamusi to the north. The city covers a total area of 40,600 km², with an urban area of 1,608 km².

Situated between the Changbai Mountains and Wanda Mountains, Mudanjiang features predominantly mountainous and hilly terrain. It is rich in water resources with the Mudan River, Wusuli River, and Muling River flowing through the region.

Historical Evolution

Mudanjiang has a long history dating back to the Neolithic Age. During the Tang Dynasty, it was the core territory of the Bohai State. The Upper Capital Longquan Prefecture ruins, located in present-day Ning'an City, are a national key cultural heritage protection site.

During the Qing Dynasty, Mudanjiang was under the jurisdiction of the Ningguta General and served as an important military and economic stronghold in the Northeast frontier. The city was established in 1937 and later became a significant industrial and tourist city in Heilongjiang Province, designated as a "China Excellent Tourist City" in 2011.

Population & Ethnicity

As of 2025, Mudanjiang has a resident population of approximately 2.2 million, administering 4 districts, 5 county-level cities, and 1 county. It is a multi-ethnic region with over 20 ethnic minorities including Manchu, Korean, Hui, and Mongolian, with Korean and Manchu being the most populous groups.

The Korean ethnic group primarily resides in Ning'an and Hailin, preserving unique cultural traditions and customs. The Manchu people are indigenous to Mudanjiang and possess a rich heritage of folk culture.

Economic Development

Mudanjiang is the economic center of southeastern Heilongjiang, with an industrial system centered on tourism, timber, food processing, and foreign trade. Tourism is one of the city's pillar industries, receiving over 10 million visitors annually.

Sino-Russian trade is a distinctive advantage of Mudanjiang. The city possesses national first-class ports such as Suifenhe and Dongning, serving as important channels for China-Russia trade. Major exports include agricultural and light industrial products, while imports consist mainly of timber and energy resources.
